3. Expansion of the Universe
The Qur’an states that the universe is continuously expanding, a fact confirmed by modern cosmology.
Quranic verse 51:47
4. Orbits of Celestial Bodies
The sun, moon, and heavenly bodies are described as moving in their own orbits, not stationary.
Quranic reference 21:33
5. Barrier Between Seas
It mentions a barrier between bodies of saltwater and freshwater, which oceanography later identified as haloclines.
verse (55:19- 20, 25:53)
6. Mountains as Stabilizers
Mountains are described as pegs, helping stabilize the Earth’s crust, similar to modern geological understanding of tectonic roots.
Quranic reference 78:6,7
7. Atmosphere as Protection
The sky is described as a protective ceiling, consistent with the role of Earth’s atmosphere in shielding life. reference 21:32
8. Pain Receptors in Skin
The Qur’an indicates that pain is associated with the skin, which modern medicine confirms through nerve endings.
Quranic reference (4:56)
9. Iron Sent Down
Iron is described as being sent down, aligning with the modern understanding that much of Earth’s iron came via meteorites.
Quranic reference 57:25
10. Fingerprint Uniqueness
It mentions the ability to reconstruct fingertips, highlighting their uniqueness confirmed by forensic science.
Quranic reference 75:3–4
11. Darkness in the Deep Sea
The Qur’an describes layers of darkness in deep oceans, matching what science discovered about light absorption underwater. (24:40)
12. Time Relativity
It refers to different measures of time, which resonates with modern concepts of time relativity.
